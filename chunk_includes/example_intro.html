Example

In this HIT you will see 65 items like the following, each presenting two pairs of categories:


Which pair of categories are more related to each other?

   wheel
A circular component that is intended to rotate on an axle bearing.
  ↔  car
A motor vehicle used for transporting up to eight people.

   building
A structure with a roof and walls standing more or less permanently in one place.
  ↔  crane (type of bird)
A family of large, long-legged, and long-necked birds.



Be careful: words in this HIT can sometimes refer to multiple categories! For instance, "crane" could mean a lifting machine or a type of bird. In this case, we mean the type of bird, and you should answer accordingly.

In this example you would probably choose pair 1, because the categories wheel and car seem more closely related than the categories building and (the type of bird!) crane.

Don't know the meaning of a word? Use your mouse to hover over a word to see its definitions.

In this HIT, often there will be a clear difference in relatedness between the pairs. Sometimes both pairs will be highly related, or both relatively unrelated -- in these cases always choose the pair that is slightly more related.

PART 1. INFORMATION SHEET

Purpose of the research

  • The research aims at collecting human judgments of category relatedness, to see how well different computational models of categories align with humans, with the aim of coming to a better understanding of how people use language, with the possible long term goal of improving human-computer communication.

Procedure description

  • You will be presented with around 100 items. Each item consists of two pairs of categories. In each item you have to choose the pair of categories that are more related to each other.
  • This HIT contains control items to monitor the quality of your work. We reserve the right to block the participation in the project if your answers to these control items are unsatisfactory, e.g., evidence of random answering; in this case, you will not be reimbursed.
