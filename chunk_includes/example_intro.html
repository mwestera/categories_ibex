This HIT is about whether various words are related to each other in meaning.

Example:

  1. wheels ↔ car: these words are closely related, because cars have wheels.
  2. queen ↔ crane: these words are not closely related.
When comparing pair 1 vs. pair 2, you would say that pair 1 is more closely related than pair 2.

In this HIT you will see around 50 comparisons like this. Every time you have to choose the pair of words that are more closely related to each other.


Sometimes both pairs contain words related in meaning. In such cases you have to pick the pair with the more strongly related words.

Example:

  • wheels ↔ car vs. dog ↔ race: the first pair should be preferred as every car has wheels but not every dog is involved in a race.


It is also possible that neither pair contains closely related words. Then you have to pick the pair that contains at least slightly more related words.

Example:

  • cup ↔ asphalt vs. auction ↔ car: the second pair should be picked because at least fancy vintage cars can be sold at auctions.
